Spider-Man: Into the Spider Verse

 
 

Introduction

Before we dig in, let's start with the film's synopsis

Bitten by a radioactive spider in the subway, Brooklyn teenager Miles Morales suddenly develops mysterious powers that transform him into the one and only Spider-Man. When he meets Peter Parker, he soon realizes that there are many others who share his special, high-flying talents. Miles must now use his newfound skills to battle the evil Kingpin, a hulking madman who can open portals to other universes and pull different versions of Spider-Man into our world.

 

CHARACTERS

 

Miles Morales

Miles Morales, voiced by Shaimek Moore, plays the main Brooklyn boy of the animated movie. He's a teenager from Brooklyn who was bitten by a Genetically Altered Spider and became Earth-1610's Spider-Man following the death of that reality's Peter Parker.

Gwen Stacy

The amazing Spider-Woman from another dimension, Gwen Stacy, was voiced by Hailee Steinfeild. At a young age, Gwen was bitten by a radioactive spider and, using her newfound spider-like abilities to fight crime, became known as Spider-Woman. She is one of the Spider-People who were accidentally pulled out of her dimension by the Kingpin's Super-Collider.

Peter B. Parker

Peter from another dimension, just with the middle name 'B.', was voiced by Jake Johnson. Parker is the one and only Spider-Man of his universe. His life as a vigilante/superhero caused a toll on his body and other aspects of his life, resulting in a rather worn-out version of Peter.

Spider-Noir

From the Nazi era, Spider-Noir, was voiced by Nicolas Cage. He's the Spider-Noir who loves killing Nazis during his era and drink egg creams. Just unfortunately got dragged into the the world of Miles Morales, a far more colorful dimension than his.

Peni Parker

Peni was voiced by Kimiko Glenn. Peni Parker is a young Japanese-American girl who, with a robot and a spider, protects the New York of Earth-14512 as SP//dr. Just like Spider-Noir and the rest, she got dragged into the dimension of Brooklyn.

Peter Porker

Porker from the toony world was voiced by John Mulaney. Also known as Spider-Ham, he is an anthropomorphic and comedic version of Peter Parker, originating from the cartoonic world of Earth-8311.
